Urban Heat Island & Green Cover Dynamics — Delhi

A spatio-temporal climate analytics study assessing land surface temperature and vegetation loss in Delhi using Landsat 8 satellite-derived indicators.

Remote Sensing Landsat 8 LST · NDVI Climate Analytics Spatial Statistics Python

Overview

Executive Summary

Delhi's rapid urban expansion has intensified the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect, increasing surface temperatures and reducing vegetation cover. This project quantifies spatial thermal intensification and evaluates its relationship with vegetation decline using multi-year satellite data.

The analysis demonstrates a clear inverse relationship between greenness and heat — with the densest built-up districts recording the highest land surface temperatures and sharpest NDVI declines over the study period.

Context

Problem Statement

Rising surface temperatures impact public health, energy demand, and urban resilience. This study investigates three core questions:

  • How has land surface temperature evolved spatially across Delhi's districts?
  • Which districts exhibit the highest thermal vulnerability?
  • Is vegetation decline correlated with temperature intensification?

Data Sources

Data & Inputs

  • Landsat 8 Collection 2 Level-2 Surface Reflectance (NASA/USGS)
  • Derived Land Surface Temperature (LST) — thermal band processing
  • NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) — bands B4 & B5
  • Delhi administrative boundary shapefiles — district level

All imagery was atmospherically corrected and analysis-ready. Images were filtered for low cloud cover (<10%) across comparable seasonal windows.

Methods

Technical Workflow

1

Preprocessing and quality filtering of Landsat 8 imagery — cloud masking and band selection

2

Computation of NDVI and LST rasters using thermal infrared and near-infrared bands

3

Raster clipping to Delhi administrative boundary shapefile

4

Zonal statistics aggregated at the district level for comparative analysis

5

Multi-year trend analysis to detect temporal intensification patterns

6

NDVI–LST correlation assessment using Pearson and spatial regression methods

Results

Key Findings

Central Delhi shows the highest surface temperature intensification across the study period.

Peripheral districts demonstrate stronger vegetation resilience, with higher NDVI values persisting.

A clear inverse relationship between NDVI and LST is observed — lower greenness correlates with higher temperatures.

Thermal hotspots spatially align with dense built-up areas, confirming the UHI signature.
